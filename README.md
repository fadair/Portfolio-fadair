I am experienced in managing complex datasets for large scale projects, developing systems for data validation, and documenting methodologies for cross-disciplinary geospatial outputs across Great Britain and internationally. My work often revolves around Environmental Impact Assessments and so I have become capable with UK constraints data, site selection, rasters, metadata, and custom methodologies.

During my degree I specialised in biogeography by completing field-based research including soil–tree interactions, environmental impacts on dune systems, and erosion processes in arid landscapes. I also accompanied Chester Zoo as a field agent for a month-long reconnaissance of Gabon’s coastal, riparian, secondary, and primary rainforest. That involved extensive footwork, remote camping, and UAV-supported habitat identification.

In my own time I like to create maps and recently I have contributed my own professional QGIS layout templates to GitHub and the QGIS Layout Hub. In 2025 I also published a literature review on Equatorial Africa Forest Conservation that I had begun as an object of personal study while working with the Africa Field Programme.
